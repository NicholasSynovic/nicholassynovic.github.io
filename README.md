## About

Nicholas M. Synovic is an AI Application Architect and Computer Science
Researcher at Loyola University Chicago (LUC) where he conducts empirical
research on the intersection of software engineering, AI, and deep neural
network (DNN) reuse.

Nicholas is driven to identify *patterns*, *methods*, and *techniques* to
leverage AI models within existing applications in domains by expanding and
improving upon methods to operationalize them.

He has achieved his Bachelor's and Master's degrees (in 2022 and 2024
respectively) in Computer Science from LUC.

His professional work history spans:

- Website developement, publication, management, and maintence,
- Development and deployment of distributed cluster computers for computer
  vision research, and
- Developing applications for assisting in internal talent management and
  development.

His academic history includes:

- 6 academic publications at top software engineering confrences,
- 3 academic confrence committee positions,
- 2 preprints on the challenges and advances of the pre-trained DNN reusability,
  and
- Is a two time panelist at Intel oneAPI events and ambassador

Nicholas M. Synovic's resume is availible upon request.
